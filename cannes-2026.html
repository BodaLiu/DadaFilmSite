Drawing on reporting from Variety, The Hollywood Reporter, Screen Daily, Deadline, IndieWire, and IONCINEMA, this page offers a ranked prediction of likely selections for the 2026 Cannes main competition. The official lineup is due on April 9, and the notes below reflect the state of the race in early April.

I

Tier I: Almost Certain

6 titles

These films combine strong completion signals with directors who already have deep ties to Cannes. Unless one of them shifts to another festival or runs into a late technical problem, this is the strongest cluster for the main competition.

1
Bitter Christmas Bitter Christmas
Director: Pedro Almodóvar Cast: Cate Mara, Rooney Mara
A Spanish-language return described as a gendered tragicomedy, already released in Spain in March. After six previous Palme runs without a win, Almodovar is widely seen as one of the year's strongest contenders. Sony Pictures Classics has North American rights.
Six previous Palme bids Spanish-language return
2
Fjord Fjord
Director: Cristian Mungiu Cast: Sebastian Stan, Renate Reinsve
Mungiu's first English-language feature centers on cultural conflict between two families in a remote Norwegian village. Every one of his previous features premiered in Cannes, NEON has picked up North America, and several outlets are treating selection as close to inevitable.
Palme d'Or winner English-language debut
3
All of a Sudden All of a Sudden
Director: Ryusuke Hamaguchi Cast: Virginie Efira, Tao Okamoto
Set in Paris, the film follows two scholars moving from academic exchange to intimacy. Hamaguchi returns to Cannes with a French-language project after the Oscar success of Drive My Car, with NEON on distribution.
Oscar-winning director
4
Parallel Tales Parallel Tales
Director: Asghar Farhadi Cast: Isabelle Huppert, Catherine Deneuve, Virginie Efira, Vincent Cassel, Pierre Niney
An unusually stacked French ensemble gives this project major prestige weight. If selected, it would mark Farhadi's fifth time in the main competition. Charades is handling international sales.
Two-time Golden Bear winner
5
Sheep in the Box Sheep in the Box
Director: Hirokazu Kore-eda
A dystopian new work from Kore-eda that continues his interest in family and childhood from a darker angle. It is set for a June release in Japan, with NEON attached, and looks like the more clearly positioned Cannes play among his 2026 projects.
Palme d'Or winner Genre shift
6
Un Bon Petit Soldat Un Bon Petit Soldat
Director: Stéphane Brizé Cast: Vincent Lindon, Alba Rohrwacher
Brize and Lindon reunite after their long-running run of French social dramas, including the performance that won Lindon Best Actor at Cannes. Screen Daily singled it out as one of the strongest French contenders in the field.
Cannes Best Actor team-up
II

Tier II: Very Likely

6 titles

This group has strong industry buzz and real momentum, but still carries some uncertainty around premiere strategy, final delivery timing, or exact section placement.

7
Minotaur Minotaur
Director: Andrey Zvyagintsev
A major comeback project from the two-time Oscar-nominated Russian director, who has not released a feature in nearly a decade since Loveless and spent a long period recovering from severe COVID. The return alone makes this one of the season's major events.
Two Oscar nominations Return after a decade
8
Her Private Hell Her Private Hell
Director: Nicolas Winding Refn Cast: Thatcher, Calva, Melton, Frostes
Refn returns to feature filmmaking a decade after The Neon Demon. It is positioned as a secretive thriller, with very little public detail beyond the cast and tone.
First feature in ten years
9
Gentle Monster Gentle Monster
Director: Marie Kreutzer Cast: Lea Seydoux, Catherine Deneuve
Kreutzer looks like a serious candidate to move up from Un Certain Regard-level positioning into the main competition. mk2 Films is handling sales, a useful signal given its recent Cannes track record.
10
The Beloved The Beloved
Director: Rodrigo Sorogoyen Cast: Javier Bardem, Victoria Luengo
A father-daughter estrangement drama with strong talent on both sides of the camera. Sorogoyen's profile has been rising steadily, Bardem has major Cannes stature, and the film appears to be ready for launch.
11
Full Phil Full Phil
Director: Quentin Dupieux Cast: Woody Harrelson, Kristen Stewart
A surreal comedy about an American billionaire in Paris trying to reconnect with his daughter, pitched as a warped answer to Emily in Paris. Dupieux is a regular Cannes presence and this one feels scaled up in a noticeable way.
12
Paper Tiger Paper Tiger
Director: James Gray Cast: Adam Driver, Scarlett Johansson, Taylor
A hard-edged crime film that has reportedly been submitted to Cannes. Gray is one of the festival's core American auteurs, with five previous premieres there, though some reporting still suggests a degree of uncertainty over the final launch plan.
Festival route still unclear
III

Tier III: Strong Contenders, Real Variables

6 titles

The directors here all carry enough prestige to matter, but post-production timelines, editing complexity, and the possibility of waiting for another festival window are all meaningful variables.

13
1949 1949
Director: Paweł Pawlikowski Cast: Wheeler, Dyer, Madley
A black-and-white postwar road film about Thomas Mann. Backed by Mubi, but the shoot reportedly wrapped only in mid-December, leaving a very tight post-production schedule, and Pawlikowski has hinted that 2027 remains possible.
Oscar winner for Ida Tight post schedule
14
Jack of Spades Jack of Spades
Director: Joel Coen Cast: O'Connor, McDormand, Manville, Lewis
A Scottish gothic mystery set in the 1880s, and Coen's second solo feature after the brothers split. It wrapped in autumn 2025 with Bruno Delbonnel as cinematographer, but the post-production timeline remains the biggest question mark.
Palme-winning pedigree Tight post schedule
15
Coward Coward
Director: Lukas Dhont
A World War I trench drama about cowardice and heroism. Dhont's first two features both launched in Cannes, with Girl in Un Certain Regard and Close in competition on the way to an Oscar nomination.
Oscar-nominated filmmaker
16
Hope Hope
Director: Na Hong-jin Cast: Hwang Jung-min, Cho Seung-woo, Hoyeon, Fassbender, Vikander
A science-fiction thriller reportedly mounted at one of the largest budgets in Korean film history, set around a mysterious discovery near the DMZ. Na is said to be imagining it as a larger franchise, and reports suggest the edit is especially complex.
Return from The Wailing director Complex edit
17
After After
Director: Lars von Trier Cast: Stellan Skarsgard
A possible farewell work by von Trier, made while living with Parkinson's and reportedly shot in a nursing-home setting with a stripped-down visual approach. If it makes the lineup, it would instantly become one of the festival's most emotionally charged premieres. Reports suggest it is complete.
Palme d'Or winner Possible farewell film
18
Out of This World Out of This World
Director: Albert Serra Cast: Riley Keough, F. Murray Abraham
Serra's first English-language feature follows an American diplomat heading to Russia against the backdrop of the war in Ukraine. It wrapped last autumn, but the film appears to require a substantial amount of post-production work.
English-language debut Tight post schedule

Summary

This year's field may be lighter on major Hollywood studio spectacle, but the auteur concentration is unusually strong. The possible lineup includes names such as Almodovar, Mungiu, Kore-eda, von Trier, and Coen, alongside major returning figures like Farhadi, Zvyagintsev, Hamaguchi, and Na Hong-jin, plus younger directors still consolidating their place in the festival ecosystem. The biggest uncertainty is post-production timing: if films by Pawlikowski, Coen, Na, or von Trier slip, the final shape of the competition could change significantly.
